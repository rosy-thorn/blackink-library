quite night

I put my hands behind me on the kitchen counter, jump slightly, and sit on it. I get my glass from next to me, then fill it from the tap nearby. Then I open the drawer under me with my foot and, using my toes, lift my syrup out and bring it up to my hand.

God, I love this. It's just like when I was a kid. It's been ages since I've done this. I feel so light, and I'm smiling. My music is still playing in my ear as I pour some syrup into my glass and just leave the bottle next to me.

Swinging my legs, feeling like I'm on cloud nine, I pick up my glass and wrap my hands around it like it's hot milk, then take a sip. Just perfect flavoring, as always. Mum always makes it too weak.

I'm so tall sitting up here, listening to my music and feeling like my real age — 16 — instead of feeling like I'm 20. No thoughts. No worries. Nothing. Silence. For once, it's my time. No emails, DMs, messages, calls, chores. Just before bed.

My boyfriend walks in, saying, "Hey," and steps between my legs, rubbing the outsides of my thighs.

"Everything okay?"

I smile. "Perfect."
